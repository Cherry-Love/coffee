Delving into the history of the unique fragrant drink, we are returning to the streets of Lviv again. It was here, back in 1829, that the first coffee house was opened. Its founder is considered to be Yuri Kulchitsky, a native of a Western Ukrainian village, who helped liberate Vienna from the Turks and received 300 bags of coffee as a gift. Kulchitsky taught Europeans to drink an invigorating drink, and now, according to his unique old recipes, “Kava z Lvova” is made at the Lviv plant.

The main suppliers of raw materials for the plant were coffee plantations from India, Brazil, Ethiopia and Colombia.

Thanks to the latest technologies and qualified specialists brought from Germany, Spain, India and Brazil, this plant, the first in Ukraine, has the ability to produce 2,000 tons of coffee per year.

This Ukrainian coffee is not prepared at a large factory - it is roasted by a married couple from Kamyanets-Podilskyi using secret technologies. The recipe and the bean supplier are kept in strict confidence, however, Alexey and Natalia convince that it is 100% Arabica.

The whole charm of a small enterprise is that the manufacturer does not roast the beans for the future - coffee is prepared individually for each customer. Products can be ordered through the website.
